			Existence Theorem: At least one object with a certain property actually exists. ∃x such that P(x),
						An existence theorem does not always give a formula or method to construct the object. This is similar to saying there is at
						least one seat in the stadium that is empty.<----your not pointing to it, your only claiming such a seat exist.
						Ex: There exist an integer that is even.
							Take 2, 2=2⋅1, so it is even, so ∃n∈ℤ such that n is even<-----in this logic notation/compact mathematical language,
							
							∃n∈Z such that n is even
							∃=there exist<-----it's called the existential quantifier,
							∃n=there exists an n,
							n is an integer, ℤ is the set of all integers: ..,-2,-1,0,0,1,2,3,..											
							so n∈ℤ = n belongs to the integers.

				Rational Numbers:
						Are that the number can be written as a fraction of two integers, with the denominator not equal to zero(can be written as a fraction).
						Ex:	0.5 or 1/2, 0.75 or 3.4, 0.333.. or 1/3
				Irrational Numbers:
						Are that of which that the number in decimal form cannot be written as a fraction of two integers(cannot be written as a fraction).
						Ex:	√2 or 1.41421356.., √3 or 1.73205080.., π or 3.14159265.., e or 2.71828182

				Direct Proof:			You start with what you know and logically work to the conclusion.
				Proof by Contradiction:		Assume the statement is false and show that this leads to an impossibility.
				Proof by Induction:		Used when a statement is claimed to be true for all positive integers.
				Proof by Cases:			Split the problem into several possibilities.
				Proof by Counterexample:	To disprove a statement, find one example where it fails.

				Trominoes:	A tromino is a shape made from 3 equal squares joined edge-to-edge. 3 squares in an 'L' shape
